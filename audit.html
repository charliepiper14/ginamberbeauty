Funnel Insights

Top of Funnel (Impressions to Click): CTR at 1.20%

Your CTR sits slightly below the 1.5 percent benchmark for DTC skincare. This suggests creative hooks could be stronger, or targeting may be too broad in prospecting campaigns. The positive indicator is that retargeting campaigns show much higher engagement, confirming warm audiences respond well to your messaging.

Click to Landing Page: 10.56% Drop Rate

You are losing 1 in 10 visitors between clicking the ad and the landing page loading. This exceeds the ideal range of 5 to 8 percent. Potential causes include slow page load speed on mobile, tracking discrepancies between Meta and Shopify, or users accidentally clicking and bouncing. Recommendation: Run PageSpeed Insights on key landing pages and review mobile experience.

LPV to ATC: 48.10% (Exceptional)

Nearly half of all landing page visitors are adding to cart. This is phenomenal for ecommerce where the typical benchmark is 8 to 12 percent. Your product pages, pricing, and offer are extremely compelling. This validates that traffic quality from ads is strong when visitors reach the site.

ATC to IC: 50.94% (Excellent)

Half of cart adders proceed to checkout. This indicates strong purchase intent and minimal cart abandonment at the consideration stage. Your cart page is performing its role effectively.

IC to Purchase: 35.93% (Primary Opportunity Area)

You are losing nearly 2 out of 3 people at checkout. The benchmark for optimised ecommerce is 50 to 60 percent. Potential friction points include shipping costs (especially international), checkout flow complexity, limited payment options, or final price shock. This represents the biggest leak in your funnel and the most significant opportunity for improvement.

Funnel Summary: Your mid funnel (ATC to IC) is exceptionally strong, showing that people who engage with your products genuinely want to buy. The two areas requiring attention are: (1) getting people to the landing page efficiently (click to LPV drop), and (2) checkout completion. Addressing checkout abandonment alone could increase conversions by 30 to 40 percent without additional ad spend.

What the Attribution Gap Tells Us

The 0.98x ROAS Discrepancy Explained

The gap between Facebook's 2.21x and Triple Whale's 1.23x is significant but expected. This happens because:

  • View through attribution: Facebook counts users who saw an ad but did not click, then converted within 1 day
  • Multi channel journeys: User sees Meta ad leaves returns via email/Google/direct Triple Whale credits last touch
  • iOS 14.5 limitations: Some conversions cannot be tracked back to Meta due to privacy changes
  • Assisted conversions: Meta is doing awareness work that other channels are getting credit for

The data suggests Meta Ads are functioning primarily as a demand generation and awareness engine rather than a direct response channel. This is actually appropriate for your business because:

  • Microneedling requires education before purchase (not impulse buy)
